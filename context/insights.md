# insights

- **2026-04-24 (cost-arithmetic, critical):** BTC 15m cost in R-units = `(bps_rt / 10000) * close / atr`. Mean close/atr on BTC 15m over 2020-2024 is **357** (p50=281, p95=844). Therefore each 1 bps of round-trip fee ≈ 0.036 R per trade. At 7bps RT (maker entry + taker exit + 1bps slip each) real per-trade cost = 0.25R, not 0.14R. Any hardcoded "cost = 0.13R at 12bps" is wrong for BTC 15m. **Rule:** always compute cost per trade as `(bps_rt/10000) * close/atr`, not as a fixed R constant — otherwise conclusions are biased toward over-optimism in consolidation regimes (2023-2024) where close/atr balloons.
- **2026-04-24 (MACD-crossover survives where mean-rev dies):** Unlike BB mean-reversion (2020-2021 regime only, dead after), MACD-crossover 15m on BTC is **strengthening over time**: per-year raw mean fwd_ret_r is +0.15 (2020), -0.04 (2021), -0.18 (2022), +0.47 (2023), +0.21 (2024). ML filter at prob_thr=0.55 amplifies 2024 to +0.39R net at 7bps fees. Interpretation: momentum-of-momentum signals are in a more favourable regime than mean-reversion as market structure matures post-2022 (less retail-driven overshoots). This is a live-data signal worth paper-trading.
- **2026-04-24 (ledger filter bias):** Default `--max-cost-r 0.5` in `build_signal_ledger.py` drops trades where per-trade cost > 0.5R. Since close/atr grows in consolidation, the filter disproportionately drops recent-year trades (2023-2024) where ratios reach 400-800. This biases back-tests against the strongest recent regime. **Fix:** disable or raise for current analysis (`--max-cost-r 99`), or model the cost exclusion policy explicitly in the deployment strategy.
- **2026-04-24 (walk-forward per-fold per-year essential):** Pooled metrics lie when ML filters stop trading in later folds. BB-15m @ prob_thr=0.65 shows pooled mean +0.148R, hiding fold 4 trading 0 out of 1,776 signals. Always report per-fold AND per-year breakdown; if any fold trades 0 while pooled is positive, the strategy is regime-dependent and does not generalize forward. Minimum bar for deployability: ≥3/5 folds and ≥3/5 years with positive expectancy, most recent year included.
- **2026-04-24 (framing bug, important):** Conclusions about "signal has no edge" drawn from ledger gross-R with `net_r > 0` labels can be **wrong** when the exit model is bad. BB-15m ledger shows gross-R ≈ −0.015 with 3ATR-stop/3ATR-target exits, yet raw 16-bar forward R on the same signal rows shows mean +0.04R, median +0.34R, WR 56.8% vs random 50.8%. Lesson: before concluding "signal is random", always check the signal's **raw forward return distribution** vs a random control. The stop/target simulation can destroy real edge by cutting trades that would recover within the horizon. Use forward-return labels first, then layer exit optimization.
- **2026-04-24 (fee-boundary artifact — scope-corrected):** MCC of LightGBM filter **rises monotonically with round-trip fees** when the label is `net_r > 0` downstream of a stop/target-destroyed ledger: (-0.017 @ 2bps) → (+0.026 @ 7bps) → (+0.018 @ 12bps) → (+0.050 @ 24bps). Interpretation: the model learns the fee boundary, not alpha. Implication: high MCC on a `net_r > 0` label does not imply profitability. Switching the label to `fwd_ret_r > 0` (exit-model-free) gave a cleaner training signal and surfaced a real +0.15R filtered mean at prob_thr=0.65 on 7.7% coverage.
- **2026-04-24 (BB-15m edge quantified):** BB mean-reversion on BTC 15m has a weak but real edge: per-trade Sharpe ≈ 0.013 (mean 0.04R / stdev 3.09R over 16 bars). Taker fee drag (0.13R) = 0.042 Sharpe-units = **3.2× the alpha Sharpe**. No intraframe stop/target exit can fix this (8 designs tested, all negative gross) because the signal's +0.34R median requires 16-bar holding to emerge and intermediate drawdowns cut it off. The edge is only capturable with (a) horizon-only exits and (b) ML filter to concentrate on high-probability cells (prob≥0.65 lifts mean to +0.15R) and (c) near-zero execution cost (maker-only).
- **2026-04-24 (exit-design insight):** On noisy 15m BTC with signal Sharpe ≈ 0.01-0.02 per trade, **trailing stops are strictly worse than fixed stops** (A6 trail WR=65%, gross −0.09R vs A5 fixed target WR=63%, gross −0.05R). Trailing stops lock in early pullbacks within runs. **Horizon-only exit extracts the most gross-R** (A1 gross +0.0005) because the signal's edge is distributed across the full horizon, but needs zero fee drag to survive.
- **2026-04-24 (feature audit):** 129 indicator features carry weak but real directional signal on BTC 15m (AUC 0.542 mean walk-forward on sign(fwd_16bar), MCC +0.055). Not zero but not strong. Adding more TA-style indicators unlikely to materially improve; microstructure/orderflow data would be the next tier.
